About Mohamed Elsawaf

PhD Candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering

Research Focus

Mohamed Elsawaf's research advances RF, microwave, and terahertz technologies for next-generation communication systems and biomedical applications. His work focuses on innovative approaches to substrate-integrated waveguides, mode-division multiplexing, and concurrent dual-mode excitation techniques.

His contributions have established him as an emerging leader in high-frequency circuit design, with multiple publications in IEEE journals and conferences demonstrating novel solutions for modern communication challenges.

Academic Journey

Mohamed earned his B.Sc. (2018) and M.Sc. (Hons.) (2021) degrees in Electrical Engineering from Ain Shams University, Cairo, Egypt. During his graduate studies, he served as a Research Assistant in the Microwave and Antenna Research Lab (MARL).

His research potential was recognized by the USC Graduate Research Fellowship (2021), supporting his doctoral work at the University of Southern California.

Industry Experience

Before doctoral studies, Mohamed worked as a Software Development Engineer on the Device Modeling team at Siemens EDA (formerly Mentor Graphics), focusing on RF simulation and semiconductor device modeling.

This industry experience bridges academic innovation with commercial implementation and informed several applied-research projects.

In the US, Elsawaf was an intern at Skyworks Solutions Inc., and his work on developing integrated passive components is patented.
