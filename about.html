About me:

Dr. Mohamed Elsawaf received the B.Sc. and M.Sc. (Hons.) degrees in Electrical Engineering from Ain Shams University, Cairo, Egypt, in 2018 and 2021, respectively.

Following graduation, he worked for three years as a Software Development Engineer in the Device Modeling team at Siemens EDA (formerly Mentor Graphics). Concurrently, he served as a Research Assistant in the Microwave and Antenna Research Lab (MARL) at Ain Shams University.

He is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Southern California (USC), Los Angeles, CA. His research focuses on passive and active microwave and terahertz (THz) components for communications and biomedical applications.

Dr. Elsawaf was awarded the USC Graduate Research Fellowship in 2021.
